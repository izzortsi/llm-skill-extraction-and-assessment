# First Self-Described Anarchist

## Question

A student claims: 'William Godwin was the first political philosopher to formally identify himself as an anarchist.' Based on the passage, is this claim correct or incorrect?

## Passage

The first political philosopher to call himself an anarchist () was Pierre-Joseph Proudhon (1809–1865), marking the formal birth of anarchism in the mid-19th century. Since the 1890s and beginning in France, libertarianism has often been used as a synonym for anarchism and its use as a synonym is still common outside the United States. Some usages of libertarianism refer to individualistic free-market philosophy only, and free-market anarchism in particular is termed libertarian anarchism.

## Expected Output

incorrect

## Acceptance Criteria

### Must Identify

- Pierre-Joseph Proudhon was the first to call himself an anarchist
- William Godwin contributed to anarchist doctrines but did not use the term anarchist to describe himself
- The formal birth of anarchism is marked in the mid-19th century by Proudhon

### Correct Conclusion

incorrect
